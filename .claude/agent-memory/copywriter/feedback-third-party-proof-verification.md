When a brief asks to use a third-party official listing (distributor site, certification registry, directory) as proof of authenticity for a client claim, do not assume every field on that listing is current just because the listing itself is legitimate. Cross-check each specific detail (address, phone, name) against the client's confirmed current NAP/contact data in MEMORY.md before writing copy that states they "match exactly."

**Why:** Caught a real case with SensiSkin — a Revelo (HydraFacial distributor) partner listing was solid proof of legitimate partnership, but its address field was the client's OLD address (already flagged as needing correction across other directories in a prior NAP audit), not the current one. The phone number did match. Writing copy that asserted "address AND phone both match exactly" would have been factually wrong on the address, even though the source itself was genuine and the phone match already proved the point.

**How to apply:** For proof-of-legitimacy copy, prefer citing only the fields you've verified as current-match (e.g. phone number) rather than the full data. Flag any stale field found in a third-party source to the client for correction, but don't let it block delivering copy — just narrow the claim to what's actually verified.
