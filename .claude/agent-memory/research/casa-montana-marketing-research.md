Casa Montana marketing strategy scouting completed 2026-07-18 (three-layer
approach: global luxury chalet leaders, budget STR tactical marketing,
Kopaonik-region local competitors). Full report:
outputs/casa-montana/research/marketing-strategije-2026-07-18.md in the
SensiSkin repo. This was scouting material for the owner to pick 4-6
strategies from — not a finalized plan, so don't treat any single strategy
listed there as "decided" without checking [[casa-montana-marketing-research]]
follow-up or the client's own MEMORY.md for what the owner actually chose.

**Non-obvious findings worth remembering for future Casa Montana work:**

- Villa Prive and Spa (the competitor named alongside Casa Montana on
  J2Ski as a leading Kopaonik luxury chalet) has no confirmed own branded
  website — it only surfaces through a white-label OTA directory page
  (visitaserbia.com) plus Booking/Airbnb. This means Casa Montana's planned
  dedicated site is a real differentiator versus its closest reputational
  peer, not just a "nice to have."
- Kežman Mountain Houses is the most marketing-sophisticated Kopaonik
  competitor: own professional site, explicit "Be direct with us" book-
  direct incentive + newsletter capture on the homepage, seasonal promo
  codes (Black Friday, Early Bird), and a live-embedded Instagram feed with
  weekly Reels (~8.5K followers) leaning on destination/mood content rather
  than room tours. Worth revisiting as the closest realistic tactical
  reference once Casa Montana's site exists.
- Global luxury chalet "leaders" (Firefly Collection, Oxford Ski Company,
  Consensio Chalets) are agency/portfolio operators with 700-1000+
  properties and decades of staff — most of their visible tactics (press
  walls, concierge teams, loyalty programs) do NOT scale down to a single
  property. Only the underlying principle transfers: sell the experience/
  trust, not the bed, and put one clear "why book direct" message up
  front. Be careful not to over-index on these brands' specific tactics in
  future Casa Montana strategy work — they're aspirational reference, not
  a tactical playbook, for a single-property client at this budget.
- Firecrawl scrape observation: oxfordski.com's homepage markdown was
  heavily polluted with an unrelated "video downloader" widget's repeated
  boilerplate (dozens of duplicate "$2.99/month Pro subscription" blocks) —
  worth skipping past that noise if re-scraping this URL rather than
  assuming it's real page content.
