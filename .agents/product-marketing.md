# SensiSkin — Product Marketing Context

## Brand overview
SensiSkin is a skincare brand focused on sensitive skin. Products are formulated 
for people who struggle with redness, irritation, breakouts, or reactions to 
conventional skincare. The brand philosophy is: simple ingredients, visible results, 
no compromise.

## Products
- Core line: gentle cleansers, barrier repair moisturizers, calming serums
- Positioning: clinically-tested, dermatologist-approved, fragrance-free
- Price point: mid-to-premium ($35–$95 per product)
- Format: e-commerce first, DTC via sensiskinstudio.com

## Target audience
**Primary**: Women 25–45 with sensitive or reactive skin
- Frustrated by products that promise results but cause flare-ups
- Research-oriented — reads ingredient lists, follows skincare communities
- Values transparency and brand authenticity
- Has likely tried and been disappointed by "natural" and mainstream brands

**Secondary**: Skincare-aware men 28–40 dealing with post-shave sensitivity

## Brand voice
- Tone: calm, confident, expert-but-approachable
- Style: direct and informative without being clinical or cold
- Personality: the knowledgeable friend who happens to be a dermatologist
- Avoid: overpromising, fear-based messaging, overly technical jargon
- Use: "skin" not "complexion", "works" not "transforms", "gentle" not "mild"

## Key differentiators
1. Formulated specifically for sensitive/reactive skin (not just "gentle for all")
2. Transparent ingredient sourcing and testing
3. No fragrance, no dyes, no common irritants — ever
4. Results visible within 2–4 weeks with consistent use

## Customer journey
1. Awareness: social, word-of-mouth, search ("best moisturizer for sensitive skin")
2. Consideration: ingredient research, reviews, comparison with competitors
3. First purchase: usually starter kit or single hero product
4. Retention: repurchase cycle 6–8 weeks, email/SMS key retention channel
5. Advocacy: reviews, UGC, referral

## Competitive landscape
- Direct: CeraVe (mass), La Roche-Posay (pharmacy), Vanicream (clinical)
- Indirect: "clean beauty" brands positioning as gentle (Tatcha, Drunk Elephant)
- Differentiation angle: SensiSkin is built FOR sensitive skin, not just "gentle enough for"

## Key metrics to track
- CAC (target: <$45)
- LTV (target: >$180 over 12 months)
- Email open rate (benchmark: 35%+)
- Conversion rate (target: 3.5%+ on PDPs)
- ROAS on paid (target: 3.5x+)
- Repeat purchase rate (target: 40%+ within 90 days)
